Clinical AI needs boring excellence

Evaluation, source fidelity, and workflow fit.

Draft placeholder. This page will hold a longer essay on why the useful parts of clinical AI are often quiet, measurable, and operational.

The hard part is rarely the demo. The hard part is knowing when the system is wrong, how wrong it is, who notices, and whether the workflow gives people enough time and context to correct it.

Boring excellence means traceable inputs, conservative claims, clear review surfaces, and evaluation that matches the real clinical task.
